ðŸ“œ Old-Fashioned Methods

Start with One Good Paper

  1. Find a Key Source

    Identify a foundational or particularly relevant paper for your topic, such as:

    • A paper frequently cited in your area of interest
    • A work by a prominent thinker in the field
  2. Mine the Bibliography

    Read through the paper's references to find additional sources. You'll often discover:

    • Related debates or counterarguments
    • Key historical texts or foundational works
  3. Trace the Citations

    Use tools like Google Scholar or Connected Papers to see who has cited this paper. This helps you follow the evolution of the debate over time.

Use the Library

Browsing the philosophy section of the library often reveals texts you wouldn't otherwise think to search for. Sometimes the best ideas come from nearby shelves.

What Is It?

Undermind is an AI-powered tool for academic research. It helps you:

  • Find relevant academic papers quickly
  • Summarize complex ideas into clear insights
  • Explore connections between debates in your field

Important: YOU HAVE FIVE FREE SEARCHES BEFORE YOU HAVE TO PAYâ€¦ so use them wisely!

How to Use Undermind

  1. Sign Up

    Visit Undermind and create an account.

  2. Tell it who you are and what your assignment is

    Be clear and as specific as you can be.

    Example:

    "I want to investigate philosophical perspectives on stigma in mental health care. I'm thinking that _____ might be a good way to open this investigation. I'm an undergraduate, and the essay I'm working on is a 10-page paper that must use a handful of secondary sources."

  3. Analyze Results

    Review the suggested papers and their summaries. Focus on relevance and context.

  4. Build a Bibliography

    Export Undermind bibliography in a .ris file format.

ðŸ“š Building Your Bibliography with Zotero

Zotero is an essential tool for managing your research and creating citations. It helps you:

  • Save and organize articles directly from your browser
  • Generate citations in multiple formats (APA, MLA, Chicago, etc.)
  • Create a bibliography with just a few clicks

How to Use Zotero

  1. Download Zotero

    Visit Zotero and install both the application and the browser extension.

  2. Create a folder for your class
  3. Import the .ris file from Undermind

    You can also drag and drop PDFs from other sources, or look them up via their DOI or ISBN (or you can manually build your entries).

  4. Generate Your Bibliography

    When you're ready, Zotero can export your citations into a perfectly formatted bibliography.

    Download the Zotero extension for your preferred writing platform (Word, GoogleDocs, LaTex) and use it to build your citations.
