About Me

I'm a Ph.D. candidate at Vanderbilt University bridging classical Islamic philosophy with contemporary AI ethics and philosophy of mind. My research explores how historical philosophical frameworks can inform our understanding of artificial intelligence and machine reasoning, while using AI tools to advance research in classical philosophy.

Areas of Specialization

  • Classical Islamic Philosophy
  • Philosophy of AI
  • Ancient Philosophy
  • Political Philosophy
  • AI Ethics

Current Research

RPW Center Graduate Researcher on Generative AI

Leading initiatives on AI applications in humanities research and teaching AI & Humanities faculty seminar.

Classical Text Analysis with LLMs

Developing innovative approaches to source analysis in classical Arabic, Syriac, and Greek texts using large language models, aiming to transform traditional philological methods.

Democratic Discourse Enhancement

Creating AI-powered tools to improve democratic discourse by identifying and elevating meaningful but overlooked contributions in public discussions.
