About Me

About My Research

I am a Ph.D. student in Philosophy at Vanderbilt University with a research program crystallizing around issues in AI ethics and the philosophical frameworks that inform them. As a student, my work is still wide ranging, dealing with issues around rationality, AI alignment, and ethical frameworks that underpin these systems. I'm particularly interested in exploring the ways in which language models resemble human cognition and the use of these models in stress-testing philosophical assumptions.

Beyond theoretical frameworks, I develop open-source tools for ethical evaluation of AI systems and lead interdisciplinary initiatives as Co-Director of "AI and the Human" at Vanderbilt. My research combines philosophical depth with practical applications, creating methodologies that bridge technical implementation with normative reasoning. As my research program evolves, I maintain a consistent focus on frameworks that characterize how AI influences human cognition, evaluate representational harms in AI systems, and integrate philosophical insights with technical approaches.
